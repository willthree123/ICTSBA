Disadvantages

1. Security

In order to succeed full automation, each product was assigned different permission. The main iot system may be safe enough, but there are some vulnerable branches which can’t guarantee it is perfectly safe. Once a product is exposed to risk, hackers may sneak into nearby connected products and take control of parts of the products. In 2016, the largest DDoS attack ever was launched using an IoT botnet. A malware called Mirai can keep searching vulnerable IoT devices via the Internet and login with default usernames and passwords. Devices such as digital cameras and DVR players were inflected and attacked the Dyn servers which control much of the internet’s domain name system (DNS) infrastructure. Causing huge portions of internet services shut down, including Twitter, Netflix and Reddit.

2. Privacy

IoT devices collect sensitive user data. During transmission, data might be intercepted, captured. Manufactures and data centres are storing tons of sensitive data. Each datum is corresponding to a analytic, which is a competitive information. It is so strategic that it encourages hackers to access those data.

  • Smart home device company Wyze has confirmed leaking personal data on the internet. 2.4 million people included users' emails, nicknames given to cameras, Wi-Fi names, health data like weight and gender, and information on users' Wyze devices.

  • In 2019, a publicly accessible database was discovered about the popular IoT management software, SmartMate which has collected over two billions user logs collected from smart home appliances. The database stored serial numbers usernames, passwords, email, IP addresses, account reset codes and GPS data for every IoT device.
